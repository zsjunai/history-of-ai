The history of Artificial Intelligence is an epic story of humanity's attempt to understand and replicate its own intelligence. From the first mathematical model of a neuron in 1943 to the large language models reshaping the world in 2026, AI's journey has been marked by dramatic swings — fervent optimism, painful winters, unexpected breakthroughs, and boundless imagination about the future.

## Why This Book

This book adopts the five-part structure of Sima Qian's *Records of the Grand Historian* (Shiji) to systematically chronicle AI history:

- **Annals**: Chronological chapters tracing each era's core debates and turning points
- **Houses**: Institutional histories of the organizations that shaped AI
- **Biographies**: Profiles of key figures — their choices, contributions, and fates
- **Treatises**: Thematic deep-dives into technical domains and societal impacts
- **Timeline**: A comprehensive chronology of AI milestones

## Why Open Source

History should not be written by one person. Through open-source collaboration, we aim to reduce bias, stay current, encourage participation, and ensure accuracy.

## How to Read

- **Chronologically**: Start with the Annals, era by era
- **By interest**: Jump to any biography or treatise
- **Quick overview**: Check the [Timeline](/en/timeline/)
